Here's all the buzz about Honey Bees

Honey bees (Apis mellifera) are highly social flying insects that live in large colonies with a single queen. They are crucial pollinators for ecosystems and agriculture, and they produce honey from nectar. The three castes of a hive are the queen, who lays eggs, the female worker bees, who build and maintain the hive and collect food and the male drones.

Beekeeping and apiculture are interchangeable terms for the practice of maintaining honey bee colonies in hives. This activity is done to harvest products like honey and beeswax, and to provide pollination services for crops."Apiculture" is derived from the Latin word for bee, apis, and is a more scientific term for the practice, while "beekeeping" is the common term used in English.

Why Honey Bees are important

Honey bees are crucial because they are vital pollinators, ensuring the reproduction of many plants, including about a third of the world's food crops, which contributes to biodiversity and food security. They also provide valuable products like honey, beeswax, and royal jelly.
